Meet our instructors

Jerry Collins

Jerry’s impressive career history in the automotive industry spans over 4 decades. Jerry’s interest in cars began when he was a young boy, helping his father restore classic cars in their back garden. During his time in the motor trade, Jerry has worked his way up from technician to owning his own garage. He is also an experienced MOT tester, having carried out thousands of vehicle inspections since the 1990s.
In his spare time, Jerry is kept busy by his 2 grandchildren, watching the rugby and competing in the weekly pub quiz.

Aaron James

Aaron has worked in the motor industry for over 20 years. His mum recalls that she knew Aaron was destined to be a mechanic when as a child, he was more interested in trying to break his toy cars and put them back together than racing them around the track! Aaron began his career as an apprentice vehicle technician as a local garage, before working for numerous leading vehicle manufacturers including Ford, Volvo and Mercedes-Benz.
Outside of work, Aaron enjoys spending time with his wife and children, long walks with his two dogs and indulging in his passion for photography.

Steve Robinson

Steve and his family recently returned to the UK after spending 5 years in Canada, where Steve worked as a mechanic in Vancouver. Steve began his career in mechanics in the 1990s, learning the trade in various garages across the UK. Prior to his move to Canada, Steve spent over 10 years with the AA, where he trained hundreds of apprentices.
Steve has a passion for travel and live music. He was a guitarist in a band during his university days, and meets up with his former band mates to play music together as often as he can.

Becky Ludlington

Becky is a former student of Clutch Kings College. She enrolled with us aged 16 after ‘being obsessed with cars’ all her life. After graduating, Becky worked full time as a mechanic for 5 years, including 2 years spent working for Audi. Now Becky splits her working life between being an instructor at Clutch Kings College and continuing with her mechanic role.
Becky’s love of cars extends outside of the workshop. In her free time, she loves motorsport racing, a hobby which has taken her to events in many countries including Italy, Germany, France, Austria and Japan. When she isn’t racing around the world, Becky enjoys going to the cinema and playing video games.

Hannah Shipman

Hannah is a graduate of Clutch Kings College. After studying law at university and working as a paralegal for 2 years, Hannah decided to follow her passion for cars and enrol on our Level 3 Diploma in Light Vehicle Maintenance. After completing the course, Hannah worked for BMW for 6 years before returning to Clutch Kings College as an instructor.
Hannah loves the great outdoors and her many hobbies outside of work include surfing, paddleboarding, cycling and hiking.
